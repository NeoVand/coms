# Sven Mattisson

## In one sentence
Sven Mattisson is the Swedish electrical engineer who, working alongside Jaap Haartsen at Ericsson in Lund in the mid-1990s, designed the analog RF and CMOS silicon that made the original Bluetooth radio cheap enough to put inside a consumer phone.

## The hook (cold-open)
Every Bluetooth chip that has ever shipped is two engineers' work fused onto one piece of silicon. Jaap Haartsen designed the digital baseband — the hopping pattern, the piconet, the clock alignment. Sven Mattisson designed the other half: the analog radio-frequency front end and the CMOS implementation that turned the baseband design into a manufacturable integrated circuit. In 2015, the European Patent Office shortlisted the two of them together as Lifetime Achievement finalists for the European Inventor Award. The radio reached consumer price points because of the silicon decisions Mattisson made.

## The work

### Ericsson Lund — the analog half of the Bluetooth radio
The Bluetooth project at Ericsson Mobile Communications in Lund split along a clean technical line. Haartsen owned the digital baseband — covered in detail in the Bluetooth episode. Mattisson owned everything below it: the analog RF front end, the low-noise amplifier, the mixer, the frequency synthesiser that had to retune sixteen hundred times a second to follow the hopping pattern, the power amplifier, and the CMOS process work that put all of it on one die. The brief was a short-range, low-power, cheap radio in the 2.4 gigahertz unlicensed band. "Cheap" is the word that did the most work. It is the reason the radio ended up in earbuds and keyboards rather than in a rack-mounted base station. Mattisson's IC-level decisions are the reason the bill of materials came down to where a phone manufacturer would actually integrate it.

### Lund University and the IEEE Journal of Solid-State Circuits
Alongside the Ericsson work, Mattisson held an Adjunct Professorship at Lund University and was a long-running contributor to the *IEEE Journal of Solid-State Circuits* on low-power radio design — the academic venue where the analog and mixed-signal CMOS techniques that make consumer wireless economics work get published. The journal track is where the textbook version of the design philosophy lives.

### After Ericsson
Mattisson's career ran from Ericsson Research into Sony Mobile, before he retired. The source material does not detail what he shipped at each stop, so we will leave it at that — the production he is remembered for is the silicon he co-designed in Lund.

## Awards and recognition
In 2015, the European Patent Office named Mattisson — jointly with Jaap Haartsen — a Lifetime Achievement finalist for the European Inventor Award. The citation recognised the pair as the joint architects of the Bluetooth radio.

The radio that ended up inside roughly every phone, headset, and earbud on the planet is the fusion of those two halves on a single die.
