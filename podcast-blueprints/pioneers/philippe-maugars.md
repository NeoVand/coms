# Philippe Maugars

## In one sentence
Philippe Maugars is the French electrical engineer who, across more than thirty years at Philips Semiconductors and its spin-out NXP, led the reader-side and protocol work that turned NFC from a paper draft into the contactless reader chips now sitting inside payment terminals, transit gates, and access readers worldwide.

## The hook (cold-open)
In June 2015 the European Patent Office handed its Industry-category European Inventor Award to two engineers — a Frenchman and an Austrian — for jointly inventing NFC. The Frenchman was Philippe Maugars, working out of the Philips, then NXP, site in Caen in Normandy. By the time the award arrived, Maugars had been at the same company for more than thirty years, his name was on roughly twenty-five patents, and the reader chips he had designed were the ones the rest of the world's contactless ecosystem was talking to.

## The work

### Three careers inside one company
Maugars's career is a single-employer story spread across three distinct technical arcs, all run out of the Philips and then NXP site in Caen, France. He worked on smart-card readers, then on power management, and then on LED-based smart lighting. Roughly twenty-five granted patents came out of those three arcs. The continuity matters: the contactless reader work was not a one-off, it was one chapter inside a thirty-year integrated-circuit career.

### The reader side of NFC
Maugars's central contribution is on the reader side of the standard. Where his co-inventor Franz Amtmann — there is a separate episode on him — owned the physical-layer and integrated-circuit architecture out of the NXP site in Gratkorn, Austria, Maugars owned the reader-side and protocol work out of Caen. The mechanism itself — the 13.56-megahertz inductively-coupled radio at roughly ten centimetres of range, the payment, transit, identity, access, and tap-to-pair commissioning modes — belongs to the NFC episode. What matters here is the production: the contactless reader chips that ship today in essentially every payment terminal, transit gate, and access reader running on NXP silicon are direct descendants of Maugars's reader designs out of Caen.

### The 2015 award and the partnership with Amtmann
The European Patent Office's 2015 European Inventor Award, in the Industry category, went jointly to Maugars and Amtmann, specifically for jointly inventing NFC. The pairing is the whole story of how the standard shipped: a wireless air-interface protocol does not become a billion-unit shipping product without both the silicon-and-physics side, which Amtmann owned in Gratkorn, and the reader-and-protocol side, which Maugars owned in Caen. The two sites worked the problem from opposite ends and met in the middle.
