# Preeti Ohri Khemani

## In one sentence
Preeti Ohri Khemani is the Infineon Technologies executive who, as the current Chair of the NFC Forum, is the public face of the Release 15 jump from a five-millimetre to a twenty-millimetre certified operating volume — the change she has called the technically hardest standardisation effort the Forum has taken on while staying compatible with the ISO 14443 contactless cards already in the field.

## The hook (cold-open)
For the first fifteen years of certified NFC, the working assumption was a five-millimetre tap — phone almost touching the reader. In June 2025, on Khemani's watch as Chair of the NFC Forum, that number quadrupled. Release 15 took the certified operating volume out to twenty millimetres, and Khemani went on the record describing it as the hardest piece of standardisation the Forum had ever shipped — because the new range had to land without breaking the ISO 14443 cards already running the world's transit gates and payment terminals.

## The work

### Chair of the NFC Forum
Khemani is the public Chair of the NFC Forum as of the 2025 Release 15 announcement, succeeding the prior leadership line that ran from Koichi Tagawa through Mike McCamon as Executive Director. The Chair role at a standards body is the spokesperson seat — the person who has to explain to the press, to the member companies, and to the regulators why the next release matters. For Release 15 that meant defending an aggressive technical change without losing the installed base.

### The 20 mm operating volume
The headline of Release 15, in June 2025, is a quadrupling of the certified operating volume from five millimetres to twenty millimetres. That is the change Khemani has been the public voice of, and the change she has called the technically hardest standardisation effort to date. The constraint that made it hard is the same constraint that has shaped NFC since the beginning: it has to stay compatible with ISO 14443, the contactless smart-card standard that the world's transit and payment infrastructure is already built on. The protocol mechanism — the thirteen-point-five-six-megahertz inductive coupling, the operating modes, the transit and payment use cases — belongs to the NFC episode. What Khemani's tenure adds is the production: a four-times bigger usable volume, certified, without invalidating the cards already in everyone's wallet.

### Wireless charging, digital product passports, and NDEF
Three other things have moved through the Forum on Khemani's watch. NFC Wireless Charging — the second-generation spec known as WLC 2.0 — was promoted to an IEC standard in March 2026. The NFC Data Exchange Format, NDEF, was driven to formal IEC standardisation in the same month, March 2026. And in March 2025 the Forum published the candidate spec for the NFC Digital Product Passport, NDPP, the tap-to-read identity layer aimed at the European product-passport regulations.

### The 2026 roadmap and Multi-Purpose Tap
Looking forward, Khemani's Forum has committed its 2026 Technology Roadmap to Multi-Purpose Tap, MPT, as a strategic direction. The bet is that a single tap from a phone should be able to authenticate, pay, open a door, hand off a Wi-Fi credential, and read a product passport, instead of asking the user to choose an app first.
