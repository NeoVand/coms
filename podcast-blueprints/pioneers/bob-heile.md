# Bob Heile

## In one sentence
Bob Heile is the standards engineer who turned the IEEE 802.15.4 low-power radio into an industry — co-founding the Zigbee Alliance in 2002 and chairing IEEE 802.15 for almost two decades, until days before his death in 2020.

## The hook (cold-open)
Pick up a Philips Hue bulb, an IKEA Trådfri remote, or a Walmart shelf label, and the radio inside it is descended from a working group that one man chaired for nearly twenty years. In August 2002, Bob Heile co-founded the Zigbee Alliance and ran it through 2013, growing it from a concept into an organisation of more than four hundred member companies. He was still chair of IEEE 802.15 in September 2020 when prostate cancer took him, in North Attleboro, Massachusetts. The chair role was passed days before he died.

## The work

### From physics to BBN
Heile took a physics doctorate at Johns Hopkins. He started his data-communications career in 1980 at Codex Corp., and in 1997 he joined BBN to commercialise wireless technology. From there he transitioned full-time into the standards work that would define the rest of his career.

### IEEE 802.11, from the founding
Heile was a founding member of IEEE 802.11 in 1990 — the working group that produced Wi-Fi — and he stayed an active contributor to it for the rest of his life. The Wi-Fi mechanics belong to the Wi-Fi episode; what matters here is that he was in the room when the group started.

### The Zigbee Alliance, August 2002
The contribution he is most identified with is the Zigbee Alliance, which he co-founded in August 2002 and chaired through 2013. Zigbee is the IEEE 802.15.4-based low-power mesh radio that ended up inside Philips Hue lighting, IKEA Trådfri, and the Walmart electronic shelf labels you walk past in every store — the protocol mechanics live in the Zigbee episode, and the chapter on Zigbee, Thread, and the Matter bridge tells the story of how the family fits together. Under Heile's chair, the Alliance went from a founding handful to over four hundred member companies.

### IEEE 802.15 and 2030.5
Concurrently, Heile chaired IEEE 802.15 — the working group on Wireless Specialty Networks, the home of 802.15.4 itself — for almost two decades. He also chaired IEEE 2030.5, the Smart Energy Profile 2.0 standard. Almost every low-power 802.15.4-based protocol you can name — Zigbee, Thread, WirelessHART, Wi-SUN — traces its institutional lineage back through the working group he ran.

### Wi-SUN Alliance, from 2015
From 2015 he served as Director of Standards at the Wi-SUN Alliance, the industry group behind the field-area mesh networks now used in smart-meter and smart-city deployments — while still chairing 802.15 in parallel.
