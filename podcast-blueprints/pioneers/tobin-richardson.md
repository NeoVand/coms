# Tobin Richardson

## In one sentence
Tobin Richardson is the standards-body executive who took the Zigbee Alliance through its hardest pivot — launching Project CHIP with Amazon, Apple, and Google in December 2019, rebranding the Alliance to the Connectivity Standards Alliance in May 2021, and keeping Zigbee semantically alive across the Matter transition.

## The hook (cold-open)
In December 2019, the Zigbee Alliance announced a project that, on paper, looked like it might end the Zigbee Alliance — a smart-home interoperability layer co-founded with Amazon, Apple, and Google. The CEO who steered that announcement, and the rebrand to Connectivity Standards Alliance that followed eighteen months later, was Tobin Richardson. Today the organisation he runs has more than 850 member companies in 45 countries, and Zigbee, Matter, RF4CE, and Aliro all sit under one umbrella.

## The work

### Smart Energy and the path in
Richardson did his undergraduate at UC Davis and his master's at Georgetown. He entered the IoT world in 2008, helping establish Zigbee Smart Energy as the utility-industry connectivity standard — the home-area-network profile that utilities adopted for smart meters and demand-response. That utility-side work is what brought him into the Zigbee Alliance orbit in the first place.

### Joining the Alliance leadership, 2014
He joined the Zigbee Alliance leadership in 2014 and rose to President and CEO. The protocol mechanics — IEEE 802.15.4 mesh, application profiles, the Hue and Trådfri and Walmart-shelf-label deployments — belong to the Zigbee episode. What matters here is that he inherited an alliance whose radio was already inside hundreds of millions of devices, and whose biggest strategic question was whether smart-home would consolidate on Zigbee, on Thread, on something Apple-native, or on none of the above.

### Project CHIP, December 2019
The answer he organised was Project CHIP — Connected Home over IP — announced in December 2019 with Amazon, Apple, and Google as co-founders alongside the Alliance. CHIP became Matter. The story of how Matter actually works as a smart-home interoperability layer over Wi-Fi and Thread, and how it relates to the older Zigbee stack, is the chapter on Zigbee, Thread, and the Matter bridge. Richardson's role was the political one — getting the three platform owners who normally compete on smart-home into the same standards body, with the same IP terms, on the same release cadence.

### Rebranding to Connectivity Standards Alliance, May 2021
On 11 May 2021, the Zigbee Alliance rebranded to the Connectivity Standards Alliance — same legal entity, broader remit. The new CSA stewards Zigbee, Matter, RF4CE, and Aliro under one umbrella. Membership grew past 850 companies in 45 countries on his watch. The bridge code that lets a Zigbee Hue bulb show up in a Matter controller — present in Hue, Aqara, and SmartThings hubs — is the direct downstream outcome of keeping Zigbee semantically alive through the transition rather than letting Matter quietly replace it.

### World Economic Forum
Outside the Alliance, Richardson sits on the World Economic Forum's Council on the Connected World, where he chairs the WEF initiative on cross-stakeholder IoT collaboration. The thread across the work is the same: cross-vendor IoT only ships when someone keeps the room together.
